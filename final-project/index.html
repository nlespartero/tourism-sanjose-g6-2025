About San Jose

San Jose is a coastal municipality in Negros Oriental, Philippines, situated about 14 kilometers from the provincial capital and known for its clear waters and scenic natural environment. Established on May 9, 1955 through Republic Act No. 1212, the town was created from the former settlement of Ayuquitan Viejo and has since developed into a peaceful community with a strong connection to nature. Covering a land area of 54.46 square kilometers, San Jose is composed of 14 barangays and recorded a population of 21,956 in the 2020 Census, giving it a population density of 403 inhabitants per square kilometer. The municipality is often described as a charming and quiet destination, serving as a convenient gateway to major natural attractions such as the Twin Lakes of Balinsasayao and Danao. Its economy and culture reflect a mix of traditional rural life and growing environmental awareness, as studies show households contribute the most biodegradable waste, while general stores and recreation centers are among the top contributors of recyclables. Overall, San Jose continues to balance its natural beauty, cultural roots, and community development as it grows.
